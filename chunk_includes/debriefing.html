In this experiment, we’re investigating how people evaluate sentences that contain or omit goals, and how this is affected by the previous goal information provided in context. For example, you may have seen a sentence with a goal like (1), or one where the goal is omitted like (2):

 

(1) Julia led her to the library.

                                               

(2) Julia led her without issue.

 

You were asked to evaluate these sentences based on the context provided, which might have introduced a goal like (3), or might have left the goal neutral, like (4).

 

(3) Kylie wanted to go to the library, but needed directions.

 

(4) Kylie wanted to return some books, but needed directions.

 

Different verbs allow goal omission to different levels, so this experiment studies the extent to which previous introduction can license goal omission across different types of verbs.
